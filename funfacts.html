Herakles Myth Fun Fact

Herakles was always a hero, his first brave feat was against two serpents that were sent by his jealous and spiteful step-mother Hera to kill him in his cradle. Herakles, even as a baby, proved himself to be capable of anything when he was successful in his first battle.

Herakles Labours Fun Fact

Herakles should have only had ten labours not twelve, but two of them didn’t count. His brother, King Eurystheus, decided that two of the tasks he had completed were not done correctly.
Firstly, when Herakles was tasked to slay the Hydra, it was as a solo task, but his nephew Iolaus assisted him making that Labour invalid as he had not done this one by himself. Secondly, while Herakles was cleaning the horse stables for King Augeas of Elis, he diverged the river to help clean the stables and accepted monetary payment for his labour. Eurystheus decided due to these two things that these labours also did not count.
Herakles taking the easy way out in those two scenarios backfired on him and ended up creating more work for himself in the end, by him having to complete twelve instead of ten labours. If not for those two labours being disqualified Herakles would have finished his labours when he stole Geryon’s cattle.

Famous Writers Fun Facts

1. The book written by Apollodorus, The Library, is a great resource for anyone looking for further first hand information about ancient Greek mythologies.

2. Euripides was known as a prolific playwrite with endless creativity having written roughly eighty to ninty plays during his lifetime. Sadly, only nineteen of those plays have survived.

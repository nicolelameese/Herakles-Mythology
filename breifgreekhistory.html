Ancient Greek Society

  • Ancient Greek society was a thriving society starting with the Mycenaeans from 1200 BCE, concluding with the death of Alexander the Great in 323 CE. During these times people viewed myths as some view the bible by connecting historical events to mythological stories, including the myth of Herakles.

Meaning of the Myth of Herakles

  • Herakles is a great hero and although he has done some unforgivable things as we see through a modern day lends, those things could be acts that were eventually forgiven. Herakles was and still is a poster boy for correcting your wrongs and that actions speak louder than words.

    He makes a phenomenal role model as he is not a perfect individual, he makes mistakes and is flawed but he is constantly working on himself to improve and make up for his wrong doings.

Mythology & Legends

Legends and myths can be classified independently, mythologists call more traditional stories legends while earlier stories set in the cosmos or include gods as main characters are seen as myths. During the Hellenistic age scholars began to write down their oral histories and many mythologists see these writings as myths or legends due to a lack of categories that these writings can fall into in the academia world. Histories and researchers should find a way to categorise the myths and stories they are studying, as written oral stories must not be interpreted literally.

Oral traditions that were later written down are the only things that is surviving when it comes to records and documentation of ‘oral histories’ as anyone who knew the original oral story or historical event has departed. Many writers of the time combined parts of oral stories of myths and legends to the written histories of events that took place, making it difficult to look at the works now and make an completely accurate analysis of what was being described and if it was all true.

Euripides

Euripides was from Salamis Island who became a playwright with stories and themes rooted in philosophical thought. He wrote a play about the myth of Herakles portraying his internal turmoil following his fits of rage and madness. He was most famously known as a “philosopher of the stage” due to his deeper look on feelings and emotions on commonly known stories/myths.

Apollodorus

Apollodorus was a Greek scholar and historian who lived from roughly 180 – 120 BCE (Before Common Era). He wrote down many stories from popular or common myths of the times in a collection called The Library. He also wrote down a detailed account of the Herakles myth, labours, and challenges.

Diodorus Siculus

Diodorus Siculus was a historian from Sicily and wrote a forty-book series, Bibliotheke (“The Library”), covering the known history from the tome of creation until roughly 90 BCE. During his written recording of history, he wrote of many Greek myths and tried to rationalize the myth of Herakles into true historical events that happened.
